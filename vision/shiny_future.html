This page represents a complete vision for where we want async to go. This vision is what we believe to be the best way to achieve the experiences that we want async to provide.

Work in progress

Note that while a lot of the steps needed are fairly clear, several of them also have significant unknowns or points of controversy. We have attempted to highlight those and expect to be working through those points as we go.

Key aspects of the future

  • üå§Ô∏è If you know sync Rust, getting started in Async Rust is straightforward (more])
    • üå§Ô∏è Mostly, you change fn to async fn, add some calls to await, and change over to other parts of the stdlib, though supporting dyn Trait requires making some choices, particularly in a no-std environment
    • üå§Ô∏è It still has that "if it compiles, it generally works, and it runs pretty darn fast" feeling
    • üå§Ô∏è Destructors and cleanup also work the same way as in sync Rust, thanks to Drop to AsyncDrop
    • üå§Ô∏è No need to write poll functions or to interact with pin except in quite specialized scenarios
  • üå§Ô∏è High-quality documentation and tutorials helps you to get started and learn the ropes
    • üå§Ô∏è The docs also identify common patterns for structuring your async programs and their advantages and disadvantages
  • üå•Ô∏è Tooling and debugger integration gives insight into the behavior of your program
    • üå•Ô∏è Easy to get a snapshot of overall acitivity (e.g. to find out what tasks or exist or why a task is blocked)
    • üå•Ô∏è Easy to see aggregate performance trends over time (e.g., number of active connections, waiting connections, etc)
    • üå•Ô∏è Easy to profile things in terms of your async tasks (e.g., to get a flamegraph of a specific connection)
  • üå•Ô∏è Variety of high-quality runtimes available in cargo, and it's easy to change between them:
    • üåßÔ∏è When you use things from the standard library, they work across runtimes automatically
    • üå•Ô∏è There are standardized, foundational traits for common operations like I/O, spawning tasks, timers
  • üå•Ô∏è Hierarchical scopes allow you to easily spawn parallel and concurrent tasks
